D&D Keywords

These are important terms used throughout our character creator and beginner guide.

Armor Class (AC)
Armor Class shows how difficult a character is to hit in combat.
Hit Points (HP)
Hit Points measure how much damage a character can take before falling unconscious.
Hit Die
A Hit Die is tied to a character’s class and helps determine maximum hit points.
Initiative
Initiative decides the order in which creatures act during combat.
Speed
Speed shows how far a character can move during their turn.
Passive Perception
Passive Perception measures how aware a character is without actively searching.
Saving Throw
A saving throw is a roll used to resist danger, spells, traps, or harmful effects.
Proficiency Bonus
A proficiency bonus is added to rolls when a character is trained in a skill, weapon, or other ability.
Ability Score
An ability score represents a character’s natural strengths in areas like Strength, Dexterity, and Wisdom.
Ability Modifier
An ability modifier is the bonus or penalty that comes from an ability score and is added to many rolls.
Class
A class defines a character’s main role, features, and style of play, such as Fighter, Rogue, or Wizard.
Subclass
A subclass is a more specialized path within a class, such as Assassin for Rogue or Champion for Fighter.
Species
Species describes what kind of being a character is, such as Human, Elf, or Dwarf.
Background
A background describes a character’s life before adventuring and provides skills, equipment, and a feat.
Feat
A feat is a special bonus or feature that gives a character extra abilities or proficiencies.
Skill Proficiency
Skill proficiency means a character is trained in a skill and adds their proficiency bonus to related rolls.
Armor Training
Armor training shows which kinds of armor a character can use effectively.
Weapon Training
Weapon training shows which weapons a character knows how to use properly.
Tool Proficiency
Tool proficiency means a character is trained to use a specific set of tools, such as Thieves’ Tools or a Forgery Kit.
Spellcasting Ability
A spellcasting ability is the main ability score a spellcasting class uses for magic, such as Intelligence or Wisdom.
Spell Save DC
Spell Save DC is the number an enemy must meet or beat to resist a spell’s effect.
Spell Attack Bonus
Spell Attack Bonus is the bonus added when making a spell attack roll.
Cantrip
A cantrip is a simple spell that can usually be cast over and over without being used up.
Spell
A spell is a magical effect a character can cast, such as healing, protection, or damage.
